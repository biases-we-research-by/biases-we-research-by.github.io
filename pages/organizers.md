<h3>Rossana Damiano</h3>
<img src="/assets/images/Rossana_Damiano.png" width="150">

<div class="bio"> 
(she/her), is an associate professor at the University of Turin. Her research interests concern the representation of affect and values in social contexts, with a focus on Semantic Web and Linked Data. She has been co-organizer of the Italian Workshop Series on Artificial Intelligence for Cultural Heritage and of the ESSEM Workshop Series. She is Deputy Coordinator of the Open Science Working Group at her University.

<h3>Lia Draetta</h3>
<img src="/assets/images/Lia_Draetta.jpg" width="150">

<div class="bio"> 
is a PhD student in the Computer Science Department at the University of Turin, with a background in linguistics. Her research focuses on under-representation and bias in data sources, and on how large language models handle rare entities and marginalized communities. She is also interested in knowledge graphs and is exploring methodologies to integrate structured knowledge into LLMs, with a focus on long-tail and underrepresented entities.
</div>

<div class="organizer">
    <h3>Ephantus Kanyugi</h3>
    <img src="/assets/images/user.png" width="150">
    <div class="bio"> 
    is a Kenyan labor organizer, data specialist, and Vice President of the Data Labelers Association (DLA) a worker-led organization advocating for the rights and recognition of data annotators, content moderators, and AI supply-chain workers across Africa. With years of hands-on experience in data annotation and digital operations, Ephantus bridges the gap between AI research and the human labor that sustains it. His work focuses on exposing the hidden structures of exploitation in the AI industry, advancing ethical and inclusive technology governance, and ensuring that the voices of digital workers inform global AI policy and regulation.
    </div>
</div>

<div class="organizer">
    <h3>Marco Antonio Stranisci</h3>
    <img src="/assets/images/marco_antonio_stranisci.jpeg" width="150">
    <div class="bio"> 
    is a researcher in Computer Science at the University of Turin, the co-founder of a NLP start-up in Italy, a former activist in the field of human rights, and a former Humanities teacher. His research interests span from bias and hate speech detection to the investigation of social factors that determine Human-AI value misalignment and human-label variation. Thanks to his background, he delivered several tutorials, presentations, and educational sessions from different perspectives and with a wide range of audiences.
